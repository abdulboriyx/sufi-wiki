# Mystical Dimensions of Islam
**Author:** Annemarie Schimmel
**Published:** 1975, University of North Carolina Press

## Summary
*Mystical Dimensions of Islam* is a comprehensive history and phenomenology of Sufism. Schimmel traces the development of Sufism from its ascetic origins in the early Islamic period to the institutionalization of Sufi orders and the flowering of mystical poetry in Persian, Turkish, and regional South Asian languages.

The book is structured into sections covering the etymology and definition of Sufism, historical outlines (Classical, Consolidation, Theosophical), the "Path" (stations and states), Sufi psychology (Man and his Perfection), Sufi Orders, and regional developments (Indo-Pakistan, Turkey).

## Key Themes
- **The Path (Tariqa):** The systematic progression of the soul through stations (*maqam*) and states (*hal*).
- **Love ('Ishq):** The central role of passionate divine love, especially in the poetry of Rumi and the martyrdom of Hallaj.
- **Annihilation (Fana):** The goal of the mystic—to lose the ego in God and subsist in Him (*Baqa*).
- **Muhammadan Light (Nur Muhammadi):** The cosmic role of the Prophet as the archetype of the Perfect Man (*Insan Kamil*).
- **Institutionalization:** The transition from individual masters to complex brotherhoods with distinct rituals and lineages.
